Experts Interviews

David Brush / Merlin Properties
Chief Investment Officer at Merlin Properties, the largest Spanish, which owns the flexible workspace brand Loom.
Full transcript available on our blog

Summary

- The impact of the virus on real estate has greatly differed depending on the segment: warehouse logistics has actually benefitted while retail has been hit the hardest. Merlin’s flexible workspaces (Loom) has actually been moderately hit given the large portion of large corporate in the building and Loom’s tenant-focused approach.
- “The days of: you buy a building, sign a ten-year lease and speak to the tenant in nine and a half years as to whether one wants to sign a new lease are gone”. At Merlin, the virus has accelerated an already-existing trend towards a more tenant-focused and proactive approach.
- “No one had a pandemic playbook for their business prior to covid-19”. The virus will change risk planning, which might benefit flexible workspaces. It may also change the priorities of technology in particular PropTech towards a stronger emphasis of safety and health.
